Ethics and governance

Neighbourhood Signals is held by governance, not by software. The system works because the rules are public, explicit, and enforced.

Governance is what keeps Neighbourhood Signals civic, not extractive.

Stewardship

Every place appoints a Neighbourhood Steward. This person holds the covenant, convenes the Brief review, and is accountable for safety boundaries.

Stewardship is a civic role, not a technical one.

The public covenant

Each neighbourhood publishes its covenant alongside the Brief. It sets out what the system will and will not be used for.

Unblinding governance

Location is revealed only when a specific agreed action genuinely requires it. All unblinding events are logged and reviewable.

Auditability

The fact of unblinding, who attended, and what action was agreed are recorded.

Misuse and remedy

Any breach of the covenant suspends the system until corrected. Neighbourhood Signals cannot quietly drift into harm.
