What Neighbourhood Signals is

Neighbourhood Signals is a public, ethically bounded listening layer that converts aggregated navigation behaviour into a shared view of need.

It sits underneath referrals, waiting lists, and acute pressure, in the layer where people hesitate, search, loop, delay, and try to self-navigate. Those attempts contain early signal.

The goal is simple, make reaching support easier than staying confused.

What it is not

It is not surveillance. It is not behaviour scoring. It is not individual profiling.

It does not identify people, infer identities, or label individuals. If something requires personal data to be useful, it does not belong here.

The Brief is written with metacognitive humility. Signals are clues, not conclusions.

How it works

A lightweight listening layer captures aggregated patterns in what people are trying to find help with. Those patterns are translated into a short Neighbourhood Brief that partners can read in minutes.

The Brief supports early coordination, prevention, and demand shaping, without asking residents to become experts in systems.

1. Listen safely aggregate only
Capture patterns of navigation and intent, without identities or personal data.
2. Publish the Brief public artefact
Make the neighbourhood view visible, readable, and shareable across partners.
3. Coordinate action gentle response
Offer simple partner actions that reduce loops and move support closer to where people are.

Why the Brief is public

Publicness is a safety mechanism. It prevents hidden dashboards, private interpretation, and quiet mission creep.

When the Brief is public, governance is lighter because visibility is built in. It also helps communities and partners align around the same shared reality.

Neighbourhood Colours

Colours are how we keep Neighbourhood Signals non-stigmatising. They are a blinding layer.

Neighbourhood Blue signal band
A clear pattern that something is forming. Enough to discuss and prepare, without naming where it is.
Neighbourhood Yellow signal band
A stronger or persistent pattern. Enough to plan a coordinated response, still blinded to place.
We stay blind to geography until it is time for action, then unblinding happens under governance, with the smallest necessary group.
How Colours and unblinding work

Why blinding matters

Most place-based data quickly turns into place-based judgement. Colours prevent that.

They keep the conversation in the right order: signal first, interpretation second, action third, and only then location, when you genuinely need it to respond.

Ethics and governance Safety by design

Safety is the product

Neighbourhood Signals is designed as civic infrastructure, not analytics. Safety is not an afterthought, it is the architecture.

Core safeguards include: no personal data, no identity inference, no private dashboards, loop protection, and a governance covenant. The Brief is written to avoid overclaiming, and to encourage proportionate, gentle action.

The sprint is the simplest possible commitment. A place listens for 30 days, publishes the first Brief, then decides what comes next.
