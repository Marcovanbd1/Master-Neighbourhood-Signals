The Public Neighbourhood Brief

The Brief is the civic artefact that turns listening into shared responsibility. It is written for humans, published for the neighbourhood, and trusted by commissioners.

It is not a dashboard. It is a public mirror.

What the Brief is

A short, readable account of what people in a neighbourhood are trying to get help with, how that is shifting, and where loops and friction are forming.

It is written with metacognitive humility. Signals are clues, not conclusions.

What the Brief is not

It does not identify people. It does not label communities. It does not rank neighbourhoods.

It never contains personal data or inferred identities.

Why the Brief is public

Publicness is a safety mechanism. It prevents private interpretation, hidden dashboards, and quiet mission creep.

When everyone reads the same Brief, coordination replaces duplication.

What it creates

A shared civic question:

“Who is responsible for responding to what our neighbourhood is telling us?”

What the Brief contains

• What is forming (signals)
• How patterns are shifting
• Where loops and friction appear
• Suggested partner responses
• A clear “what happens next”

What happens after publication

The Brief becomes the commissioning artefact. Not a slide deck. Not a pilot report. A living civic reference point.
