A public civic artefact

The Neighbourhood Brief

A short public document that turns aggregated signals into a readable view of need, written with metacognitive humility and designed to trigger gentle, coordinated action.

See a demo Brief Safety by design Ethics and governance
The Brief is public on purpose. Publicness is not marketing, it is the governance surface.

What the Brief contains

A few pages, readable in minutes.

  • Signals that are forming aggregate
    Patterns in what people are trying to find help with, and how that is changing.
  • Neighbourhood Colours blinded
    Signal strength without naming areas, until a governed moment of action.
  • Partner actions practical
    A small set of proportionate responses across NHS, council, VCS, and community.
  • The covenant visible
    The boundaries that make the system safe are printed on the Brief itself.

Why the Brief is public

Publicness is a safety mechanism. It prevents hidden dashboards, private interpretation, and quiet mission creep.

When the Brief is public, everyone can see the same thing, which keeps governance lighter and trust higher. It also makes the work legible to communities, not just institutions.

  • No private dashboards by design
    If the only view is a private analytics tool, interpretation drifts and accountability weakens.
  • Shared reality partners
    NHS, council, and VCS can coordinate because they are reading the same artefact.
  • Visible boundaries covenant
    The public can see what is included, what is excluded, and what will never be done.
If something cannot be safely public, it is usually not a Neighbourhood Signal.

What “public” does not mean

It does not mean revealing people or places.

  • No personal data ever
    No identities, addresses, demographics, or anything that can profile an individual.
  • No stigmatising neighbourhoods blinded
    Colours mask geography until action is necessary, then unblinding happens under governance.
  • No overclaiming humility
    Signals are clues. The Brief distinguishes “what we see” from “what we think it might mean”.

Suggested structure

A consistent template helps partners read quickly, and stops drift.

  • 1. The covenant front page
    What this is, what it is demonstrated from, and the hard boundaries.
  • 2. Live signals changes
    What is rising, falling, emerging, or persisting, with plain language and no drama.
  • 3. Neighbourhood Colours blinded
    Signal bands that allow discussion without naming geography too early.
  • 4. Partner actions gentle
    A small menu of things partners can do this week, with owners and time horizons.
  • 5. Notes and limitations humility
    What may be noise, what needs human verification, and what we are not claiming.

Cadence

Choose a rhythm you can sustain.

  • Sprint output day 30
    One first Brief. The purpose is evidence, not perfection.
  • Fortnightly responsive
    Best when conditions are changing fast, and partner actions are active.
  • Monthly steady
    Best for sustainability and governance, without losing signal.
