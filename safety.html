Safety is the architecture

Safety by design

Neighbourhood Signals is civic infrastructure, not analytics. Safety is not a compliance layer added later, it is the core design principle. If something requires personal data to work, it does not belong here.

The Brief is public, the rules are explicit, and the system is designed to prevent profiling by default.

The safety model

Neighbourhood Signals avoids harm by refusing to operate at the level where harm typically happens. It does not look at individuals, and it does not create private intelligence products.

Instead, it publishes a shared civic artefact that partners can interpret together, with metacognitive humility, and with clear boundaries on what is allowed.

No personal data Nothing that identifies a person, ever.
No identity inference No demographics, no segmentation, no profiling.
No hidden dashboards Public Brief replaces private intelligence views.
Blinding by design Colours prevent stigma during interpretation.
Loop protection Stops the system amplifying its own assumptions.
Governed unblinding Location is revealed only when action requires it.
In plain terms: the system is designed so it cannot quietly become a surveillance tool.

Hard boundaries

These are non-negotiable. If any of these happen, Neighbourhood Signals has been breached.

  • Targeting individuals forbidden
    No contacting, nudging, tracing, or micro-targeting people.
  • Risk scoring citizens forbidden
    No behavioural scoring, no “risk tiers”, no vulnerability ranking.
  • Ranking neighbourhoods forbidden
    No league tables, no “problem maps”, no stigmatising labels.
  • Private intelligence dashboards forbidden
    The Brief is the product. Not hidden analytics.
  • Punitive response forbidden
    Signals trigger support and coordination, not enforcement.

Loop protection

Most analytics systems create feedback loops: you measure a thing, you act on it, then the system “sees” more of it because your actions changed behaviour. Neighbourhood Signals explicitly guards against that.

The Brief is written to avoid overclaiming. It distinguishes between signal, hypothesis, and confirmed need, and it encourages low-regret actions first.

  • Signal vs noise explicit
    Patterns have to persist before they are treated as meaningful.
  • Low-regret actions first default
    Improve navigation, information, and access before escalation.
  • Publish assumptions transparent
    Hypotheses are stated as hypotheses, not facts.
The Brief should make the system more humble, not more confident.

What “safe” means here

Safety includes privacy, but it also includes stigma, misinterpretation, and the harm caused by acting too confidently.

  • Privacy safety built-in
    No personal data, no identities, no profiling surface.
  • Interpretation safety disciplined
    Metacognitive humility is a design principle.
  • Civic safety public
    Public Brief reduces hidden power and mission creep.
