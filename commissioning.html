Commissioning pathway

Commissioning Neighbourhood Signals

Neighbourhood Signals is commissioned as civic listening infrastructure, not as a private analytics tool. The unit of value is the public Brief, plus the stewardship and governance that keeps it safe.

Start with a 30-day sprint What the Brief is Safety conditions
The sprint produces the evidence. Commissioning is the response.

What you are buying

Not software, a civic capability.

  • A public Brief cadence monthly
    A consistent, readable neighbourhood view that partners can act on.
  • Stewardship local
    A named steward function that holds boundaries and convenes review.
  • Governance covenant visible
    A published covenant that prevents mission creep and protects trust.
  • Partner actions practical
    A lightweight coordination loop across NHS, council, VCS, and community.

The decision you are making

After the sprint, the commissioning question is not “do we like this”. It is “how closely do we want to listen, and how often do we want to respond”.

The tiers below are intentionally simple. They let a place choose the depth of listening and the cadence of action, without turning Neighbourhood Signals into a complex programme.

If you can say yes to one thing, say yes to listening once, then choose the tier that matches your evidence.

Commercial framing

The commissioning model is designed to feel like civic infrastructure.

  • Priced per place not per user
    This is a neighbourhood sensor, not a licence for individuals.
  • Budgetable tiers clear
    Each tier includes the Brief, safety model, and steward pattern.
  • Evidence first sprint
    The sprint turns “maybe” into “here is what we saw”.

The commissioning tiers

Four options, from light listening to anchor-level stewardship. All tiers include the public covenant and the Brief template.

We’re listening baseline
£3,000 / year
Best for: getting the Brief into a steady monthly rhythm, with minimal overhead.
  • Monthly Brief public
    A consistent public artefact that partners can read in minutes.
  • Steward pattern light
    Local named steward, covenant upheld, short monthly review.
  • Safety baseline fixed
    No personal data, no profiling, no private dashboards.
We’re responding active
£6,000 / year
Best for: when you want the Brief to drive real partner action, not just visibility.
  • Monthly Brief public
    Plus clearer “what changed” and “what to do” sections.
  • Partner action loop lightweight
    A small response menu with owners and time horizons.
  • Review rhythm fortnightly option
    Optional mid-cycle sense check when conditions shift.
We’re embedded stewarded
£12,000 / year
Best for: when Neighbourhood Signals becomes part of neighbourhood operating rhythm.
  • Fortnightly Brief public
    Or monthly with deeper stewarded interpretation.
  • Training and steward support capability
    Support for local stewards and response owners.
  • Loop protection strong
    Stronger checks against overreaction and misinterpretation.
Anchor neighbourhood lead
£20,000 / year
Best for: a place that wants to be a regional anchor and help evolve the model.
  • Fortnightly Brief public
    Plus deeper cross-partner coordination and learning.
  • Anchor stewardship shared
    Supports stewardship maturity across the place and partners.
  • Learning transfer network
    Document and share what works so other places can adopt safely.
These tiers are intentionally legible. They can be mapped to pence-per-resident to make the civic value obvious.

What makes commissioning safe

Commissioners are not just buying output, they are buying constraints. The constraints are the system.

  • Public covenant visible
    The hard boundaries are published, and remain stable across tiers.
  • No personal data non-negotiable
    If a feature needs identity, it does not belong in Neighbourhood Signals.
  • Blinding and unblinding governed
    Colours prevent stigmatising places and enforce the right order of discussion.

What a commissioner needs to do

Three steps, then it runs.

  • 1. Run a sprint 30 days
    Produce your first Brief and your local evidence.
  • 2. Choose a tier budget
    Decide the depth of listening and cadence of response.
  • 3. Name the steward local
    A single named person to convene review and hold boundaries.
If you can do those three things, you can run Neighbourhood Signals.

The delivery partner stays quiet, on purpose.

Neighbourhood CoLab CIC carries the architecture, steward patterns, and safety model between places. Locally, ownership stays with the neighbourhood partners.
