The simplest safe commitment

The 30-day listening sprint

A place listens for 30 days, publishes its first Neighbourhood Brief, then decides what comes next. No integration. No personal data. No long contract. Just a safe window into what the neighbourhood is already trying to tell you.

See what the output looks like What happens after the sprint Safety conditions
Commercially, this is why it works: the sprint converts uncertainty into evidence. Commissioners don’t have to believe, they get their own Brief.

What you commit to

One thing only, listening for 30 days.

  • Timebox 30 days
    A defined start and end, with a single published Brief at the end.
  • Boundaries safety-first
    No personal data, no identity inference, no hidden dashboards.
  • Shared reading partners
    A short review session to interpret signals and agree proportionate next steps.

How the sprint runs

The sprint is deliberately lightweight. It is designed to fit real operational life, and to produce a credible first Brief without creating a new programme of work.

Day 0, switch on setup
Confirm the public covenant, define categories to listen to, and begin capture of aggregated patterns.
Days 1–14, early signal listen
Patterns begin to form. Colours keep the view blinded to place while meaning is discussed safely.
Days 15–29, stabilise confirm
The system checks for persistence vs noise. The Brief draft is shaped with metacognitive humility.
Day 30, publish the first Brief public
The Brief is published as a civic artefact, readable by partners and the public.
After publishing, decide what next choice
The place chooses the depth of listening and cadence of response, or stops, with no lock-in.

What you get at the end

A commissioning-grade output, not a dashboard.

  • Your first Neighbourhood Brief public
    A short, readable view of what people are trying to get help with, and how it shifted across 30 days.
  • A small set of partner actions practical
    Immediate, proportionate options that reduce loops and make support easier to reach.
  • A commissioning decision point clear
    Stay light, embed, or stop. The sprint exists to make that decision safe.
The sprint is the bridge from “interesting idea” to “we can commission this as a civic layer”.

What the sprint does not do

This prevents mission creep, and makes procurement easier.

  • No individual tracking ever
    No identities, no addresses, no demographic inference, no profiling.
  • No integration work none
    It does not require GP systems, council systems, or shared records to connect.
  • No naming and shaming protected
    Colours keep interpretation blinded to place until a governed moment of action.

What you need locally

Minimal people, clear roles, short time.

  • A local steward one person
    Owns the covenant, convenes the review, and holds the boundary line.
  • A short partner review 60–90 mins
    To read the Brief together and agree proportionate next steps.
  • A place to publish simple
    A public page for the Brief, with the covenant visible.

After the sprint, commissioning becomes a response to your own evidence.
