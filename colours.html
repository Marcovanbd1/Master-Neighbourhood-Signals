A blinding layer, not a judgement

Neighbourhood Colours

Colours are a deliberate blinding layer. They let partners discuss and plan a response to signals while staying blind to which neighbourhood is being referenced. This prevents stigma, deficit narratives, and premature targeting. Colours are never used to label or rank places.

Colours keep the order right: signal first, meaning second, action third, and only then location, if location is genuinely required.

The Colours

Colours are not a severity scale. They are a shared convention for discussing signal clusters while geography stays masked.

Neighbourhood Blue blind review

A signal cluster is forming. Enough to discuss meaning, check for loops, and sense-check with partners, while staying blind to place.

Neighbourhood Yellow blind planning

A signal cluster is persistent enough to plan a proportionate response, still without revealing which neighbourhood it relates to.

If a response can be done without knowing where, we stay blinded. Unblinding is a separate governed step, not a colour.

What Colours protect against

Place-based insight often collapses into place-based judgement. Colours stop that reflex. They keep interpretation centred on meaning and response, not blame.

  • Stigma and deficit narratives blocked
    “That estate again” thinking is prevented because place is not visible during interpretation.
  • Premature targeting prevented
    You cannot jump straight to “interventions” without first agreeing what the signal means.
  • Political heat reduced
    The conversation stays on system response and support, not reputational or punitive framing.
  • Mission creep contained
    Colours reinforce the rule: signals are clues, not conclusions, and action must be proportionate.
Colours make the system safer, and they make commissioning easier, because the boundaries are built in.

The unblinding protocol

Unblinding is not escalation. It is a practical step taken only when a specific agreed action genuinely requires location. The default position is that geography stays masked.

  • Step 1, agree the signal blind
    Interpret the signal while blinded. Decide if it is meaningful, persistent, and action-relevant.
  • Step 2, define the smallest response proportionate
    Agree what you can do without location, and what genuinely depends on knowing where.
  • Step 3, unblind for the smallest necessary group minimal
    Only the people needed to act see the location, and only for the duration needed to act.
  • Step 4, record the act of unblinding audit
    The fact of unblinding is logged, with purpose, attendees, and what action was agreed.
If you can’t justify unblinding in a single sentence, you don’t do it.

Hard boundaries

These are non-negotiable. If an action breaks these, it is not Neighbourhood Signals.

  • No targeting individuals forbidden
    This is never used to identify, track, nudge, or contact individual people.
  • No ranking neighbourhoods forbidden
    Colours never become league tables, performance scoring, or “problem maps”.
  • No punitive response forbidden
    Signals trigger support and coordination, not enforcement, blame, or sanctions.
  • No default unblinding forbidden
    The system does not run in “revealed geography” mode as standard.

Colours keep the system human.
